Evaluation of Customer Orders (Python, SQLite3)

Overview

The customer orders dataset is a csv file containing 3 yearsâ€™ worth of customer orders (2015 to 2017) with 4 columns, namely: index, customer_email (unique identifier as hash), net_revenue, and year. I was tasked with computing the following:

  • Total revenue for the current year
  • New Customer Revenue e.g. new customers not present in previous year only
  • Revenue of existing customers for current year
  • Revenue lost from attrition
  • Existing Customer Revenue Current Year
  • Existing Customer Revenue Prior Year
  • Total Customers Current Year
  • Total Customers Previous Year
  • New Customers
  • Lost Customers

Methodology

After reviewing the list of tasks expected of me, I determined that storing the customer orders dataset into a database would be the most effective strategy for me given my experience and time constraints. I have experience working with databases using SQL and using Python. Furthermore, Python has the sqlite3 library which allows me to very quickly and effectively create a database, import the csv file, and perform queries on the database.
